Tsinghua Univeristy

One-bit Input Method

Co-author of "Understanding Patients in Chinese Input Interaction with Cloud based learning"

Parkinson’s disease (PD) patients with diverse conditions face challenges in interacting with smart devices due to motor impairment especially during text input tasks. Chinese is a non-alphabetic language and consists of large character set. Chinese text entry via smart devices highlights two major interaction chal- lenges for motor impaired that affect the accuracy and efficiency : 1) Multiple key presses/ strokes required to enter a single Chinese character via tiny keys in keypads, 2) The precise selection of most relevant character from the candidate list over tiny screens with shaky hands is another challenge. To address the first problem on hand tremor and rigidity of PD, patient-centered approach is em- ployed to understand special needs and design a Chinese Input Method Editor (IME) application for improving the interaction and adaptability of smart devices for intended audience. To resolve the second major issue, proposed system uses cloud based learning to rank the most frequently and relevant candidate lists of Chinese characters. A key feature is auto correction, which helps with the most suitable candidate character selection (ranked by cloud based learning) according to the user’s sentence. Hence, auto correction based on cloud learning reduces patient’s fatigue to select the candidate character and maintain efficiency and ac- curacy. Further-more, a scanning technique is used to control text input via single tap over entire keypad area. Personalization feature allows patients with varied severity level to adapt the IME by adjusting scanning speed, and visibility (font size, back ground color) according to their pace. System has been tested with 25 PD patients to evaluate effectiveness. Our results demonstrated improved preci- sion and ease of text input which highlights that a single touch IME solution with Cloud based learning can help PD patients to use smart devices with minimal physical fatigue and to improve their social connectivity.
